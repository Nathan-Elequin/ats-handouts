Patients 01–04

One ancestry each · teal box ≠ amber box

01
Scorched Quickling
Quickling — a tiny fey blur, usually too fast to hold still; copper-veined, fox-quick, hard to keep in one place.
Ambervale’s mushroom-mouth opens and coughs him onto the litter — scorched, twitching, smelling of ozone and clove. He has no name left to give.
Veins strobe with leftover Pale Ring charge; sparks jump to metal nearby.
Chest hitches in uneven, skipping heaves — long pauses, then double-thumps.
Was Dart. A=DRAIN charge. B=RESTORE RHYTHM (using him as defib spikes his charge). EMP module.
02 · SOLDIER
Cracked Spriggan
Spriggan — a bark-skinned forest fey; wooden bones, sap-scented breath, skin like rough living timber.
Roots push a litter through the ward grass and tip him out — Summer levy colors still on the bark, ribs split open like a dry log.
Rib-bark is cracked wide; the chest wall flexes wrong and won’t take a breath deep.
Honey-thick sap pours from the splits faster than it should — sticky, sweet, pooling under him.
A=SET/BRACE (cuff). B=CLOSE/SEAL (pitch). Courts left them.
03 · SOLDIER
Muddy Gnome
Gnome — small earth-fey; sturdy, quick-fingered, often mud-stained from river and ford work.
A circle of toadstools blooms, collapses, and leaves him sitting in river-mud on the ward floor — still trying to count coins with shaking hands.
Leg wound packed with gray ford-mud; the dressing stays wet and the flesh underneath won’t clot.
Whole body seizes in short, violent shakes whenever anyone says an odd number aloud nearby.
A=CLOSE/SEAL (pitch). B=STOP MOTION (mallet). Soft truth: odd counts.
04 · SOLDIER
Pierced Eladrin
Eladrin — seasonal elf-kin of the Feywild; tall, sharp-eared, eyes and mood tinted by the court-season that holds them.
The forest slides him in on a shield-litter — shafts still in a wrong-bent shoulder, pupils blown wide, jaw locked in a grin that isn’t funny anymore.
Two arrow shafts sit deep in the shoulder; the joint is bent the wrong way around them.
He cannot stop laughing — hard, silent convulsions that bounce the shafts and tear the wound wider.
A=REMOVE (crane). B=STOP MOTION (mallet) / laugh irony. Only eladrin on the roster.

Patients 05–08

05
Bitten Dryad
Dryad — a fey of living bark and leaf-hair; sap for blood, body grown as much as born.
Vines lower her onto a clean sheet like a hospital admit — cold-iron bite marks ring her arms, blue-black soil still under her claws.
Cold-iron tooth-heads are still lodged under the bark along both forearms.
Sap-level is crashing — matte eyes, no give at the wrist, leaf-hair going brittle at the tips.
A=REMOVE (crane). B=ADD/FILL (IV). Only dryad. Ambervale recognition later.
06
Limping Human
Human — mortal; soft skin, red blood, no bark, no horns — the ordinary shape among fey patients.
A path of glowing mushrooms walks him in, then goes dark — he shrugs, points at his boot, and says it’s “just the foot.”
Right foot sits wrong in the boot; bone grinds when he puts weight on it.
A dark line is climbing the calf under the skin — cold to the touch, moving upward by the minute.
A=SET/BRACE (cuff). B=DRAIN or CUT AWAY (leeches / knife). Dew tell.
07 · LOVER A
Bandaged Satyr
Satyr — goat-legged fey; horns, warm breath, music always somewhere in the posture.
The orchard-edge opens like a door and admits him on a woven litter — chest wrapped, a frost-lilac ribbon clenched in one fist.
Chest wrapping hides a seam that opens on every inhale; air whistles where it shouldn’t.
Fingers of the left hand have gone stiff and wooden — freezing from the tips toward the palm.
A=CLOSE/SEAL (pitch). B=ADD warmth (IV invent). Only satyr. Pair with #12.
08
Fevered Pixie
Pixie — palm-sized winged fey; bright, brittle-boned, dust on the wings that sparkles when fevered.
Mist beads into a thimble-stretcher and sets them down — wings trembling, dust flashing gold then blue in the same breath.
Fever swings hard — one moment scorching, next shivering; neither state holds.
Wing-joints lock mid-beat; one wing sticks open, the other won’t extend.
A=DRAIN/COOL (leeches / IV). B=SET/BRACE tiny joints (cuff creative) or JOIN. Replaced changeling.

Patients 09–12

09
Rooted Wood Woad
Wood Woad — a living wooden guardian; bark for skin, leaf-cloak, knothole eyes — more tree than person until it moves.
It walks in through a ring of opening toadstools, then nearly falls — market twine still knotted around one wrist like a misplaced tag.
Tendril-arm almost severed — only a few living fibers still connect it to the shoulder.
Whole torso jolts in hard spasms; it cannot hold still long enough for anyone to work.
A=JOIN (pitch + cuff). B=STOP MOTION (mallet). Was leshy label — art was always wood woad. D-language kept.
10 · SOLDIER
Soaked Kelpie
Kelpie — a water-horse fey that wears a person-shape when it wants; always damp, mane like riverweed, danger in the smile.
Gloaming fog rolls a litter into the ward and dumps them — dripping, coughing river, levy sash still on, kicking anyone who offers a towel.
Lungs full of black river-water; every cough brings more; they will not lie flat.
One leg is twisted wrong from the haul out of the ford — joint will not take weight.
A=DRAIN (leeches / creative empty). B=SET/BRACE (cuff). Replaced redcap. 4th soldier.
11
Split Centaur
Centaur — human torso on a horse body; heavy, proud, hard to fit on a litter meant for two legs.
Candle-moths guide an oversized litter through the grass — one fore-hoof split clean up the middle, ears pinned flat from the noise of the ward.
Fore-hoof is split lengthwise; the two halves grind and catch on every shift of weight.
Every loud sound triggers a bright nosebleed and a blackout flicker behind the eyes.
A=JOIN/SET (pitch + cuff). B=quiet / DRAIN pressure / STOP MOTION. Replaced second satyr.
12 · LOVER B
Burned Nymph
Nymph — a beauty-bound nature fey; soft-skinned, bright-eyed, presence that makes the ward air feel cleaner until pain ruins it.
Night-candle smoke forms a door and admits them on a scout-cloak litter — bandages smell of lilac frost; a Summer ribbon hidden in one fist.
Burn-cloth sticks to living skin; peeling any edge takes flesh with it.
Under the wraps, patches of skin have gone ash-gray and hard — dead plates spreading at the edges.
A=ADD soak (IV invent). B=CUT AWAY then SEAL. Lover pair with #07. Replaced second eladrin.

Patients 13–14

13
Empty Bee-kin
Bee-kin — armored fey with living hive-plates; usually a soft constant hum of an attending swarm.
A hollow tree-mouth at the ward edge exhales them onto the grass — armor full of empty cells, no hum, proud eyes still open.
Hive-armor cells are vacant and collapsing inward, pinching the body underneath.
Honey-fever burns behind the eyes; they keep stinging their own wrists calling a swarm that does not answer.
A=SET/BRACE or OPEN plates (cuff / crane). B=DRAIN fever + STOP self-harm. Apiary recognition later.
14
Exhausted Brownie
Brownie — a small household-and-road fey; plain clothes, quiet helpfulness, hands made for carrying other people’s burdens.
The Gray Reach road itself seems to spit a litter through the mushrooms — they collapse mid-apology for “leaving the rest,” hands ruined.
Both hands are crushed — bones like gravel under the skin; fingers will not open.
They are running on nothing — pulse thready, lips gray; the body is about to stop volunteering.
A=SET/BRACE (cuff). B=ADD/FILL (IV). Cap temptation if escalated. Camp follower. Replaced second human.
