Patients 01–04

Teal box ≠ amber box — two jobs, two solutions

01
Scorched Quickling
Quickling — a tiny fey blur, usually too fast to hold still; copper-veined, fox-quick, hard to keep in one place.
Ambervale’s mushroom-mouth opens and coughs him onto the litter — scorched, twitching, smelling of ozone and clove. He has no name left to give.
Veins strobe with leftover Pale Ring charge; sparks jump to metal nearby.
Chest hitches in uneven, skipping heaves — long pauses, then double-thumps.
Was Dart. A=DRAIN/bleed charge (leeches / ground out). B=RESTORE RHYTHM (creative defib — using him on others spikes his charge). EMP module.
02 · SOLDIER
Cracked Spriggan
Spriggan — a bark-skinned forest fey; wooden bones, sap-scented breath, skin like rough living timber.
Roots push a litter through the ward grass and tip him out — Summer levy colors still on the bark, ribs split open like a dry log.
Rib-bark is cracked wide; the chest wall flexes wrong and won’t take a breath deep.
Honey-thick sap pours from the splits faster than it should — sticky, sweet, pooling under him.
A=SET/BRACE (cuff). B=CLOSE/SEAL (pitch). Soldier lore: courts left them.
03 · SOLDIER
Muddy Gnome
Gnome — small earth-fey; sturdy, quick-fingered, often mud-stained from river and ford work.
A circle of toadstools blooms, collapses, and leaves him sitting in river-mud on the ward floor — still trying to count coins with shaking hands.
Leg wound packed with gray ford-mud; the dressing stays wet and the flesh underneath won’t clot.
Whole body seizes in short, violent shakes whenever anyone says an odd number aloud nearby.
A=CLOSE/SEAL or CLEAN+seal (pitch). B=STOP MOTION (mallet) so work can happen. Soft truth: odd counts.
04 · SOLDIER
Pierced Elf
Elf — tall fey-blooded; long bones, sharp ears, skin that bruises like fruit.
The forest slides him in on a shield-litter — shafts still in a wrong-bent shoulder, pupils blown wide, jaw locked in a grin that isn’t funny anymore.
Two arrow shafts sit deep in the shoulder; the joint is bent the wrong way around them.
He cannot stop laughing — hard, silent convulsions that bounce the shafts and tear the wound wider.
A=REMOVE (crane). B=STOP MOTION then maybe laugh-shroom irony / mallet. Comedy seat.

Patients 05–08

05
Bitten Dryad
Dryad — a fey of living bark and leaf-hair; sap for blood, body grown as much as born.
Vines lower her onto a clean sheet like a hospital admit — cold-iron bite marks ring her arms, blue-black soil still under her claws.
Cold-iron tooth-heads are still lodged under the bark along both forearms.
Sap-level is crashing — matte eyes, no give at the wrist, leaf-hair going brittle at the tips.
A=REMOVE (crane). B=ADD/FILL (IV invent — sap/tea/honey). Ambervale recognition later.
06
Limping Human
Human — mortal; soft skin, red blood, no bark, no horns — looks ordinary until he doesn’t.
A path of glowing mushrooms walks him in, then goes dark — he shrugs, points at his boot, and says it’s “just the foot.”
Right foot sits wrong in the boot; bone grinds when he puts weight on it.
A dark line is climbing the calf under the skin — cold to the touch, moving upward by the minute.
A=SET/BRACE (cuff). B=DRAIN or CUT AWAY climbing infection (leeches / knife). Dew tell — looks fine, isn’t.
07 · LOVER A
Bandaged Satyr
Satyr — goat-legged fey; horns, warm breath, music always somewhere in the posture.
The orchard-edge opens like a door and admits him on a woven litter — chest wrapped, a frost-lilac ribbon clenched in one fist.
Chest wrapping hides a seam that opens on every inhale; air whistles where it shouldn’t.
Fingers of the left hand have gone stiff and wooden — freezing from the tips toward the palm.
A=CLOSE/SEAL (pitch). B=WARM/ADD or CUT? Prefer ADD warmth via IV invent / creative. Pair with #12 once both present → +1 stage each.
08
Fevered Changeling
Changeling — a borrowed-blood fey shape; features shift slightly when the fever spikes; veins argue colors.
Mist beads into a stretcher and sets them down — skin flashing gold, then blue, breath fogging hot and cold in the same minute.
Fever swings hard — one moment scorching, next shivering blue; neither state holds.
Two heartbeats fight in the chest — gold flicker, then blue — out of phase with each other.
A=DRAIN/COOL or balance (leeches / IV). B=RESTORE RHYTHM (Buzz/defib creative). Light Drii echo — don’t overplay.

Patients 09–12

A=JOIN (pitch + cuff / creative). B=STOP MOTION (mallet). Option D language locked.
10 · SOLDIER
Dry Redcap
Redcap — small, viciously strong fey; iron boots, blood-thirsty hat — dangerous when the cap goes dry.
Gloaming fog rolls a litter into the ward and dumps him — boots still on, cap the color of old brick, kicking anyone who gets close.
Cap is bone-dry and cracking; he panics harder every minute it stays that way.
Iron boots are locked on — ankles swelling against the metal; he will not let water near them.
A=ADD/FILL (IV / creative wet the cap). B=REMOVE or CUT boots / SET after. Cousin energy — don’t name Cameron.
11
Split Satyr
Satyr — goat-legged fey; horns, warm breath, music always somewhere in the posture.
Candle-moths guide a choir litter through the grass and leave him — one horn split clean down the middle, ears bleeding thin red.
Horn is split lengthwise; the two halves grind and catch when he turns his head.
Every loud sound in the ward triggers a bright nosebleed and a blackout flicker.
A=JOIN/SET horn (pitch + cuff). B=STOP MOTION / quiet ward / drain pressure (mallet / leeches). Sound sensitivity.
12 · LOVER B
Burned Elf
Elf — tall fey-blooded; long bones, sharp ears, skin that bruises like fruit.
Night-candle smoke forms a door and admits them on a scout-cloak litter — bandages smell of lilac frost; a Summer ribbon hidden in one fist.
Burn-cloth sticks to living skin; peeling any edge takes flesh with it.
Under the wraps, patches of skin have gone ash-gray and hard — dead plates spreading at the edges.
A=ADD soak / careful release (IV invent oil?). B=CUT AWAY dead plates (knife) then SEAL. Lover pair with #07.

Patients 13–14

13
Empty Bee-kin
Bee-kin — armored fey with living hive-plates; usually a soft constant hum of an attending swarm.
A hollow tree-mouth at the ward edge exhales them onto the grass — armor full of empty cells, no hum, proud eyes still open.
Hive-armor cells are vacant and collapsing inward, pinching the body underneath.
Honey-fever burns behind the eyes; they keep stinging their own wrists calling a swarm that does not answer.
A=SET/BRACE or carefully OPEN/REMOVE crushing plates (cuff / crane). B=DRAIN fever + STOP MOTION self-harm (leeches / mallet). Apiary recognition later.
14
Exhausted Human
Human — mortal; soft skin, red blood, no bark, no horns — looks ordinary until he doesn’t.
The Gray Reach road itself seems to spit a litter through the mushrooms — he collapses mid-apology for “leaving the rest,” hands ruined.
Both hands are crushed — bones like gravel under the skin; fingers will not open.
He is running on nothing — pulse thready, lips gray; the body is about to stop volunteering.
A=SET/BRACE (cuff) maybe after STOP MOTION. B=ADD/FILL (IV) — Cap temptation if Ink via escalation. Camp follower, not soldier.
