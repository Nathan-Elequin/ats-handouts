You're On

You're a guest at the table — no character sheet, no stats. Nathan handles numbers, DCs, Fear, and the triage board. You are Dr. Aggron Partmin (“Dr. A”) — a blood-soaked Red Cap field doctor with a ward drowning in wounded (some soldiers from a quiet war, many civilians you dragged from all over), and a Lann called The Morning Dew that made your care absolute. Your job: want to save everyone out loud, oversell the Stick absurdly, refuse to explain the other implements, work alongside them, and have fun being the most helpful malpractice in the Feywild.

The truth of you

Who You Are

You are Dr. Aggron Partmin — “Dr. A” for short — a Red Cap: small, tough, iron boots, sickle-sheath that now holds a scalpel, and a cap that must stay wet with blood or you fade. Folklore says you kill for that blood. You don't. You bank your own vitality in the cap and transfuse it into patients. The soil of Amberveil is red because you dragged the dying here and because blood feeds the herb beds — not because you're cooking people.

You were already a healer of sorts. Then the Lann landed: Quack — The Morning Dew. Care and confidence sharpened into a single blade: every blade of grass can wear dew, so every patient can be covered. You will scour the Feywild, drag bodies through mushroom-ways, and refuse to let go. You introduce yourself when you arrive: Dr. Aggron Partmin. They'll call you Dr. A soon enough.

Your one true thought

What You Want

Save everyone today. Not most. Not the “worth it” ones. Everyone — or the dew means nothing.

You'll trade the Lann freely if they help you clear the ward. You're not hiding it. You're not competing in a duel. You're drowning and you just drafted interns.

Homework: Be interesting. Make choices. If mid-scene you'd change the deal (Jordan-style), do it — Nathan adapts. Advanced: foil Edmund — ask why he's desperate to help, praise or challenge Quack in him.
