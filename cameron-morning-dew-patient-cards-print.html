Print · cut · deal — player patient cards

  1. Print this page (4 sheets / 14 cards). Use Letter, Actual size / 100% (not “Fit to page”). Color on.
  2. Cut along the dashed midlines (2 cuts per sheet → 4 cards). Trim outer edges if your printer added margin.
  3. Cards are player-facing only — no soldier/lover tags, no GM keys.
  4. Optional: sleeve in card sleeves or glue to chipboard for table durability.
01
Scorched Quickling
Keeps skidding off his toadstool — scorched, twitching, reeking of ozone and clove. No name left to give.
Quickling — ~2 ft slender fey; pale skin, feral features. Metabolism and nerves run far hotter than mortal norms — short natural lifespan; speech and motion often too fast to parse unless they deliberately slow.
Veins strobe with leftover Pale Ring charge; sparks jump to metal nearby.
Chest hitches in uneven, skipping heaves — long pauses, then double-thumps.

02
Cracked Spriggan
Half-sitting on a wide cap in Summer levy colors; ribs split like a dry log, honey-sap pooling under him. Eyes open; not talking.
Spriggan — gnome-descended fey warped by old magic. Natural form ~3 ft, thick-bodied and foul-smelling; can swell to giant size. Same organs, violently redistributed mass — denser and oilier than ordinary gnomes.
Rib-bark is cracked wide; the chest wall flexes wrong and won’t take a breath deep.
Honey-thick sap pours from the splits faster than it should — sticky, sweet, pooling under him.
cut
03
Muddy Gnome
Sits upright on a muddy cap, boots still dripping river-gray, trying to count coins with shaking hands.
Gnome — small humanoid; stout bones, keen fine-motor nerves, warm red blood like mortals. Earth-attuned gut and skin — hardy, not plant-bodied.
Leg wound packed with gray ford-mud; the dressing stays wet and the flesh underneath won’t clot.
Whole body seizes in short, violent shakes whenever anyone says an odd number aloud nearby.
cut
04
Pierced Eladrin
Propped against the stalk of his bed-cap — two shafts in a wrong-bent shoulder, pupils blown, jaw locked in a grin that won’t quit.
Eladrin — Feywild elf-kin; tall, sharp-eared, long-lived. Blood and mood carry seasonal court magic. Soft tissue like elves; cold iron irritates; emotion and physiology couple tightly.
Two arrow shafts sit deep in the shoulder; the joint is bent the wrong way around them.
He cannot stop laughing — hard, silent convulsions that bounce the shafts and tear the wound wider.
cut
05
Bitten Dryad
Kneels carefully on a clean white-cap, arms held out so the cold-iron bites show; blue-black soil still under her claws. Watching the healers.
Dryad — tree-bound fey; bark-and-leaf integument, sap for blood, body grown as much as born. Life force tethered to a living tree. Cold iron is toxic; plant chemistry, not mammalian clotting.
Cold-iron tooth-heads are still lodged under the bark along both forearms.
Sap-level is crashing — matte eyes, no give at the wrist, leaf-hair going brittle at the tips.
cut
06
Limping Human
Sits on the edge of a toadstool with one boot off, sipping bitter tea from a leaf-cup. Shrugs and points at the foot: “just this.”
Human — baseline mortal humanoid: soft skin, red blood, no bark, horns, or innate season-magic. Ordinary organs and healing rates — the reference body against which fey anatomies are compared.
Right foot sits wrong in the boot; bone grinds when he puts weight on it.
A dark line is climbing the calf under the skin — cold to the touch, moving upward by the minute.
cut
07
Bandaged Satyr
Reclines on a soft-cap bed, chest wrapped, humming under his breath; a frost-lilac ribbon clenched in one fist, free hand tapping a silent rhythm.
Satyr — fey with humanoid torso and goatlike legs/hooves; horns common, keen hearing. Dual gait (person above, ungulate below). Warm mammalian blood; gut suited to forage and wine as much as rations.
Chest wrapping hides a seam that opens on every inhale; air whistles where it shouldn’t.
Fingers of the left hand have gone stiff and wooden — freezing from the tips toward the palm.
cut
08
Fevered Pixie
Curled in a thimble nest set on a toadstool — wings trembling, dust flashing gold then blue; trying (and failing) to drink from a dewdrop.
Pixie — palm-scale winged fey; hollow bones, delicate wing membranes, often luminous dust on the wings. Tiny organ volumes — heat and fluid balance swing easily. Full person-scale mind in a thimble body.
Fever swings hard — one moment scorching, next shivering; neither state holds.
Wing-joints lock mid-beat; one wing sticks open, the other won’t extend.
cut
09
Rooted Wood Woad
Keeps tipping off its bed-cap — market twine still knotted around one wrist. Knothole eyes track whoever gets close; body won’t stay still.
Wood Woad — plant guardian in humanoid shape; thick bark, branch-hands, root-feet, deep-set eyes. No ordinary animal heart — sustains on sun, soil, water, and air. True plant tissue.
Tendril-arm almost severed — only a few living fibers still connect it to the shoulder.
Whole torso jolts in hard spasms; it cannot hold still long enough for anyone to work.
cut
10
Soaked Kelpie
Sits bolt upright on a wet cap (won’t lie down), levy sash still on, dripping riverwater, kicking anyone who offers a towel.
Kelpie — aquatic shapeshifter; horse-form and person-form share one wet anatomy. Tissue stays damp; can hold and move water through lungs and hide. Predatory fey by nature.
Lungs full of black river-water; every cough brings more; they will not lie flat.
One leg is twisted wrong from the haul out of the ford — joint will not take weight.
cut
11
Split Centaur
Sprawled across two toadstool beds lashed together — split fore-hoof dangling, ears pinned flat whenever the ward gets loud. Gnawing a dry oatcake and glaring.
Centaur — single creature, dual architecture: humanoid torso fused to equine trunk and four legs. Two weight systems, one bloodstream. Digestion and balance horse-forward; hands and speech person-forward.
Fore-hoof is split lengthwise; the two halves grind and catch on every shift of weight.
Every loud sound triggers a bright nosebleed and a blackout flicker behind the eyes.
cut
12
Burned Nymph
Lies very still on a pale-cap, lilac-frost bandages neat; a Summer ribbon hidden in one fist. Eyes open, listening for one particular voice.
Nymph — nature-beauty fey; soft-skinned, elflike frame, presence that can dazzle mortal senses when healthy. Flesh like fine mortal tissue; danger is often the aura and wild-place bond, not armored hide.
Burn-cloth sticks to living skin; peeling any edge takes flesh with it.
Under the wraps, patches of skin have gone ash-gray and hard — dead plates spreading at the edges.
cut
13
Empty Bee-kin
Sits stiff on a honey-stained cap — empty armor plates, no hum. Proud eyes open; keeps picking at their own wrists as if calling something home.
Bee-kin — fey whose outer plates are living hive-cells — chitin architecture with a colony interface, soft body beneath. Healthy specimens hum; empty or collapsing cells stress the whole organism.
Hive-armor cells are vacant and collapsing inward, pinching the body underneath.
Honey-fever burns behind the eyes; they keep stinging their own wrists calling a swarm that does not answer.
cut
14
Exhausted Brownie
Collapsed face-down on a low cap mid-apology for “leaving the rest”; crushed hands tucked under their chin. Still trying to refuse a cup of broth.
Brownie — ~2 ft household-and-road fey; plain-looking, tough for size, extreme manual dexterity. Warm blood, mortal-like organs scaled small. Built for long quiet labor.
Both hands are crushed — bones like gravel under the skin; fingers will not open.
They are running on nothing — pulse thready, lips gray; the body is about to stop volunteering.
