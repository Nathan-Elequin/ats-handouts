How to teach

Thirty seconds each. Name it, shove it into a hand, one sentence of “what it’s for,” zero reassurance. Players may also use their own spells/experiences. These tools are distinct on purpose — don’t let two people claim the same niche without trading.

Locate
1 · The Pointing Reed
A crooked pale reed with an iron nail lashed to the tip. Twitches like a dowsing rod.
Use: Find the true hurt when symptoms lie. Point, wait for the lean, then treat what it indicates.
Tone: folksy oracle / “there it is.”
Drain
2 · Blue-Wing Leeches
Glass jar of fat leeches with iridescent blue wing-nubs. They chirp softly.
Use: Draw bad humors, “poison blood,” fever-heat, lingering magic in the veins. Better when you use an odd number.
Tone: cheerful gross.
Sedate
3 · Mercy Mallet
Short padded mallet wrapped in spice-stained cloth. Smells like cloves and regret.
Use: Instant calm / knock for invasive work. Cost: patient drops one triage stage (stabilized-but-worse). Comedy morphine.
Tone: practical violence.
Extract
4 · Trepaner's Spool
Brass hand-auger with a silk retrieval thread. Looks like an arcade claw for skulls.
Use: Head pressure, visions, spirit-splinters, “wrong thoughts,” cranial battle-magic. Operation vibes — especially funny for Edmund.
Tone: precise nightmare.

Seal
5 · Sealing Pitch
Pot of black-green sticky resin. Label in three languages, all misspelled.
Use: Close open wounds by any means — squeeze, smear, “laxative bandage,” whatever. Closure > elegance.
Tone: unhinged confidence.
Soothe
6 · Herb-and-Spice Compress
Linen poultice warm with thyme, pepperbark, and morning moisture. Smells like kitchen + clinic.
Use: Bones, breaks, cold joints, “structural” hurts. Extra strong if paired with anything pleasantly aromatic.
Tone: grandmother competence.
Cleanse
7 · Stillwater Bowl
Shallow ceramic bowl that never ripples. River water poured in goes flat and silent.
Use: Wash infection, river-curse, battlefield filth, spiritual dirt. Red Cap rule: still water only — never running river.
Tone: quiet ritual.
Bind
8 · Odd-Coin Tourniquet
Leather strap studded with three mismatched coins (never even). Jingles when tightened.
Use: Stop bleeding, bind fate, pin a soul into a body for one more round. Odd counts only — superstition that works here.
Tone: battlefield luck.
