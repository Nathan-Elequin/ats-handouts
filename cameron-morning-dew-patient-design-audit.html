Rooted Dryad — five pairs (chart layout)

Same structure each time: arrive-blurb (what a dryad is) → two presenting boxes. Conditions aim for different verbs.

Verb check used: REMOVE · CLOSE/SEAL · DRAIN · ADD/FILL · SET/BRACE · CUT AWAY · JOIN · STOP MOTION · RESTORE RHYTHM · COOL/HEAT
Option A
REMOVE + SET/BRACE
09
Rooted Dryad
Arrive

They carry her in and set her on the litter: a dryad — a fey woman of living bark and leaf-hair, sap for blood, body grown as much as born. Market twine still knots her dress hem. One forearm bends at a wrong angle. Thin green threads show under stitches along her ribs.

Intake
Presenting · 1Something alive is tracking under the rib stitches. It twitches when pressed.
Presenting · 2Left forearm hangs wrong — joint won’t load weight; bone-wood ground audibly when moved.
Option B
DRAIN + CLOSE/SEAL
09
Rooted Dryad
Arrive

Wheeled in upright, gripping the litter rail: a dryad — bark-skinned fey, leaf-hair, sap in place of blood. Face flushed dark amber. A palm-sized split in her side shines wet and will not crust over.

Intake
Presenting · 1Sap-pressure fever — skin too hot; veins stand proud; pulse hammering in the throat.
Presenting · 2Side split stays open. Every breath pushes a bead of clear fluid out; nothing scabs.
Option C
CUT AWAY + ADD/FILL
09
Rooted Dryad
Arrive

Two bearers tip her onto the ward grass: a dryad — living wood shaped into a person, leaf-crown dull and brittle. A blackened plate of bark on her thigh looks dead. Lips cracked white; no sap-sheen on the eyes.

Intake
Presenting · 1A plate of bark on the thigh is dead, soft, and spreading a gray ring into living wood.
Presenting · 2Body running dry — cracked lips, matte eyes, no give under the thumb at the wrist-sap.
Option D
JOIN + STOP MOTION
09
Rooted Dryad
Arrive

She walks in under her own power, then nearly falls: a dryad — fey of bark and leaf, sap-blooded. One vine-tendril arm hangs by a few fibers. The other hand keeps spasmodically slamming her own chest.

Intake
Presenting · 1Tendril-arm almost severed — only a few living fibers still connect it to the shoulder.
Presenting · 2Whole torso jolts in hard spasms; she cannot hold still long enough for anyone to work.
Option E
REMOVE + RESTORE RHYTHM
09
Rooted Dryad
Arrive

Dropped on a clean sheet, still damp from the river road: a dryad — bark body, leaf-hair, sap for blood. A cold-iron nail sits flush in the sternum bark. Her chest hitches in uneven, skipping heaves.

Intake
Presenting · 1Cold-iron nail driven into the chest-wood. Bark around it is black and numb.
Presenting · 2Heartwood rhythm is wrong — uneven heaves, long pauses, sudden double-thumps.
