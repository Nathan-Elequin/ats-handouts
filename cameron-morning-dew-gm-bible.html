Deal geometry: Neighbor Seams (v2.1)

Problem with center pile: grabbing from 8+ cards mid-countdown = too much reading, too much load.

Problem with “keep your two”: calm coordination; chaos dies.

Fix (Between Two Castles energy): Neighbor Seams.

  1. Four healers sit in a square/circle. Between each adjacent pair is a seam → 4 seams.
  2. Each round, place exactly 2 patient cards in each seam (8 cards total in the deal). Face-up.
  3. 30 seconds: each player may only study their two seams (left + right) = 4 cards max in attention. Item cards at seats stay face-up.
  4. Countdown from 10. On zero, per seam (resolve all four seams at once): the two neighbors each choose one card to Grab and effectively Pass the other.
    • Different picks: each takes their Grab. Clean.
    • Same pick (conflict): both roll (d6 or Duality Hope die). Winner takes the contested card; loser takes the remaining card or may refuse it → that leftover goes to Overflow (untreated / Fear magnet) and the loser is short a patient this round.
  5. Result when clean: everyone ends with exactly 2 (one from left seam, one from right). Chaos comes from conflicts and refusals, not from reading a pile.

Why this works: cognitive load is local (4 cards). No center scrum. Still punishes “both of us want the easy Gold” with rolls and possible under-staffing. Optional spice: deal a 9th–10th card into a center Overflow that only Cameron may Bark-assign after seams resolve.

Round structure

  1. Fear / Dew / blood bookkeeping from last round.
  2. Seed seams: 2 patients per neighbor gap (8). Ramp: after 4 Saved, keep ~10 in the ecosystem (extras wait in draw / Overflow).
  3. 30s read (own two seams only) → 10-count → Grab/Pass per seam → conflict rolls.
  4. Each healer with 1–2 patients: Stick + seat items + own abilities; Duality rolls.
  5. Cameron may Bark Advice / Cap Transfusion / Never Rest as before.
  6. Modules (break room, lovers, charged-quickling EMP, climax) unchanged in intent.

The Stick (universal)

Every healer forages / is “chosen by” a plain wood stick within five feet of the ward. Cameron hyperexaggerates it: wisdom-on-contact, medicinal destiny, never for sale.

Truth: it’s a poking stick. Diagnosis via poke. Optional prop: d6 “charges” that count down on use — nothing happens at zero except maybe it snaps after the 6th poke (comedy).

Not one of the eight seat items.
