The Stick · Universal

Print four copies. Cameron’s only “lesson.” Mythically oversold. It’s a stick.

Innate · finds you
Rod of Medicinal Accord
A length of pale wood, still warm from the soil. Grain that seems to lean toward suffering. The attending swears it cannot be bought — only answered when it calls your name (preferably within five feet of the ward).
“Gives wisdom to every user on the patient upon contact. The power of having been poked.”
GM: just a poking stick. Optional d6 “charges” → nothing; may snap after 6 uses.

Seat implements 1–4

Vague on purpose. Experiment. Persuade Nathan your use is good.

Ward implement
Mercy Mallet
Short wooden mallet wrapped in spice-stained cloth. Smells like cloves and apologies. The head is soft. The intention is not.
Heavy enough to change a mind — or at least a shift.
Sedate; drop one triage step when used to “stabilize.”
Ward implement
Thirstlings
A jar of fat, singing worms with iridescent blue wing-nubs. They press against the glass whenever blood is near. They look helpful. They look hungry.
Living things that take what you offer them.
Leech-kin — drain humors / fever / bad blood-magic.
Ward implement
Crystal Claw Reel
A brass-and-quartz hand-crane with silk line and a tiny grasping claw. Looks stolen from a carnival. Hums when pointed at metal or regret.
For retrieving what should not remain.
Foreign body / shrapnel / extract broadly.
Ward implement
Mooncalf Pitch
A pot of black-green sticky resin. Label illegible in three languages. Warm. Wrong. Effective-looking. Harvest notes mention “merry paddocks” and then stop.
If it closes, it counts.
Fey dung sealant — close wounds. Comedy, not forced RP gross-out.

Seat implements 5–8

Same rules. No correct answer list — only persuasion.

Ward implement
Gigglecap
A pale mushroom that faintly giggles when squeezed. Spores smell like orchard wine. Comes with a wooden spoon and no instructions.
Feed. Then something must be funny.
Patient must laugh (roleplay) for effect — anesthesia / ease / weird cure.
Ward implement
Scandal Blade
A full short-sword polished like a surgical tray. Too big for a scalpel. Too clean for a battlefield. The attending calls it a scalpel anyway.
For decisive separations.
Amputation / cut away lesions / parasites / limbs.
Ward implement
Whatever-Drip
A clear bladder on a vine-tube with a bone needle. Empty. Waiting. The tag says only: CONTENTS — YES.
Fill it with whatever the forest (or your pockets) provide.
IV — player invents fluid; you rule outcome.
Ward implement
Gearvise Bracer
A wooden-and-brass cuff with a hand-crank and too many teeth. Clicks when tightened. Looks like it could hold a bone — or a door — or a secret.
For things that must not move.
Splint / set / brace / hold in place.
