Object
Unmarked Length
About as long as a forearm. Pale wood, still slightly warm, bark scraped off in uneven patches. Light enough to hold in one fist near the middle; the thinner end tapers to a blunt point worn smooth. Tapped on a table: a dull, dry tok. No markings. No metal. A faint green smell, like snapped twigs after rain.
GM only: poking stick. Any mythic sales pitch is spoken at the table, not printed here.

Tray objects 1–4

Size, material, grip, sound, smell — nothing else.

Object
Cloth-Headed Weight
A short handle of dark wood that fits one hand, ending in a fist-sized knob wrapped in many layers of stained cloth. The cloth smells of clove and old sweat. The head gives slightly if squeezed; the core underneath is hard. Swung through the air it makes almost no whistle — soft, slow. A leather loop at the butt hangs for a wrist.
GM: Mercy Mallet — sedate; drop one triage step.
Object
Singing Jar
A clear, stoppered vessel the size of a large mug. Inside: a dozen fat, segmented creatures the length of a finger, each with tiny iridescent flaps along the sides. They climb the glass when a warm hand gets close and make a thin, continuous eee like wet fingers on crystal. The jar is heavier than it looks. Condensation beads inside.
GM: leech-kin — drain humors / fever-blood.
Object
Three-Fingered Crane
Brass body, palm-sized, with a crank on one side and a spool of pale silk line. At the line’s end: three crystal prongs that open and close when the crank turns — click, click, click. The prongs are cold and slightly sticky. Aimed at iron nails, the crystal rings once, high and short. Held in two hands: one on the crank, one steadying the body.
GM: extract foreign bodies / shrapnel.
Object
Warm Pot of Night
A lidded ceramic pot that stays warm even when the room is not. Inside: a black-green paste that stretches in strings from a wooden spatula. It smells like wet leaves and something barnyard-sweet. Labels in three scripts, all smudged. On skin it grips and will not wipe clean without oil. The spatula has a notch worn by the same grip for years.
GM: sealant (fey dung resin).

Tray objects 5–8

Same: senses only.

Object
Pale Cap with a Spoon
A soft mushroom, white-gold, about the width of a plum. The underside flutters if breathed on. Squeezed gently, it releases a puff of fine spores that smell like crushed orchard fruit. A tiny wooden spoon comes with it, stained at the tip. The stem leaks a clear, sticky bead if broken.
GM: feed; laughter required for effect.
Object
Too-Long Knife
A blade the length of a short-sword, polished hard enough to throw a single white strip of reflected lamp-light. Full tang; leather-wrapped grip for one hand, with room to choke up for a second. No blood groove — only a faint etched line of hash marks near the ricasso. Edge surgical-thin; spine thick enough to chop. In the scabbard it rattles once, metallic.
GM: amputate / cut away.
Object
Empty Bladder on a Vine
A transparent pouch, fist-sized when flat, hanging from a flexible hollow vine that ends in a hollow bone needle. Twist-cap at the top; nothing inside — pinched shut, it stays collapsed. Shaken, a tiny dry rustle comes from the vine’s inner wall. A frayed tag on the neck shows one crooked word in Common: YES.
GM: IV — player invents contents.
Object
Toothed Cuff
A hinged bracer of pale wood banded in brass, lined with felt compressed into the shape of a forearm. A side-crank turns gears visible through a little window; each click narrows the cuff a finger-width. Too many teeth on the inner ring. Locked, whatever is inside does not move. Opening it sounds like a music-box run backward.
GM: splint / set / brace.
