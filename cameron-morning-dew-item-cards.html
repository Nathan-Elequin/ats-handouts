Dewgut Choir

A thick glass jar, mug-sized, cork-stoppered. Inside: a dozen living siphon-beasts — thumb-long pale gut-bodies with wet moth-wing flaps. Flesh and slime. Silver mucus trails the walls. Smell: rain on warm stone and copper.

Magpie’s Ice-Foot

A palm-sized brass reel, oiled, smelling of storms. Wound with pale silk line. The line ends in a three-taloned foot of never-melting crystal-ice — cold, faintly sticky, sharp enough to pinch. The crank turns brass; the foot does the work.

  • Turn the crank — the ice-foot opens and closes: click, click, click
  • Open talons near iron — one high short ring, hungry for metal
