Tray · set A

Feywild junk that somehow belongs in a hospital.

Object
Hushbulb Maul

A crooked ash-root haft capped with a swollen night-bloom bulb the size of a fist. The bulb’s skin is velvet-soft and clove-sweet, but a stone pit waits under the petals. A leather wrist-loop braided from wilted stamens hangs off the grip. The bloom never quite stops quivering, as if it were still deciding whether to open.

  • Squeeze the bulb — yields like fruit, then meets a hard core that will not give
  • Swing through air — almost no whistle; the mass arrives late with a soft whumpf
GM: Mercy Mallet — sedate / knock out; drop one triage step.
Object
Dewgut Choir

A stoppered glass mug, heavier than empty glass should be. Inside crawl a dozen fat, segmented dew-guts — half snail, half siphon — with iridescent side-flaps that leave wet silver trails on the glass. Condensation beads wherever they climb. The jar smells of rain on warm stone and a faint hitch of copper.

  • Warm hand near the glass — they surge toward heat and begin a thin continuous eee, like wet fingers on crystal
GM: Thirstlings / leech-kin — drain blood, humor, fever-magic.
Object
Magpie’s Ice-Foot

A palm-sized brass body grown around a spool of pale silk line — wedding-thread, if you ask the brass. The line ends in three crystal talons: a bird’s foot carved from winter ice that never melts. The talons are cold and faintly sticky. The brass smells of oil and old storms.

  • Turn the crank — the foot opens and closes: click, click, click
  • Open talons near iron — one high short ring, as if the crystal is hungry for metal
GM: extract foreign bodies / shrapnel / lodged teeth.
Object
Mudmouth Tar

A lidded ceramic pot that stays warm when the room is not, painted with a smiling cow that has too many eyes. Inside waits a black-green paste that stretches in living strings from a notched wooden spatula. It smells of wet leaves and barnyard-sweet. Labels in three scripts circle the lid — all deliberately smudged.

  • Dab a little on skin — it grips like a mouth and will not wipe clean without oil
GM: seal / close wounds (fey dung resin). Don’t over-explain.

Tray · set B

Same tray energy. Still no purpose printed.

Object
Jubilee Cap

A soft white-gold mushroom, plum-wide, with a tiny wood spoon stained amber at the tip. The underside flutters if the air moves. The stem weeps a clear bead if broken. Spores smell like crushed orchard fruit left in sun — sweet enough to make your eyes water.

  • Squeeze gently — a puff of fine spores; something nearby wants to laugh when they land
GM: feed; laughter required for the effect to land.
Object
Etiquette Blade

A blade the length of a short-sword pretending it belongs on a tea tray. The polish throws one hard white strip of lamp-light. Full tang, leather grip with room to choke up. No blood groove — only faint hash marks near the ricasso, like a tally of apologies. The edge is surgical-thin; the spine is thick enough to chop through what manners say should stay attached.

  • Draw from the scabbard — one metallic rattle, then a silence that feels intentional
GM: amputate / cut away dead tissue.
Object
Nurse-Vine Ampoule

A transparent pouch, fist-sized when flat, hanging from a hollow living vine. The vine ends in a hollow bone needle. Twist-cap at the top. Nothing inside. A frayed neck-tag shows one crooked Common word: YES.

  • Blow into the twist-cap — pouch swells, then sighs flat; the vine drinks air toward the needle
  • Shake — a tiny dry rustle from the vine wall, as if something once lived there
GM: IV — player invents fluid contents; you rule.
Object
Kindjailer’s Briarvise

A hinged bracer of pale wood banded in brass, lined with felt pressed into a forearm’s ghost. A side-crank turns gears in a little window. Too many briar-teeth crowd the inner ring. It looks like jewelry that learned cruelty and then apologized.

  • Turn the crank — each click narrows the cuff a finger-width until whatever is inside cannot move
  • Open the hinge — sounds like a music-box run backward
GM: splint / set / brace / hold still.
