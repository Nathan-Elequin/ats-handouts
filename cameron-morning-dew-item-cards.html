Tray · set A

Feywild junk that somehow belongs in a hospital. Purpose is for testing.

Object
Hushbulb Maul
  • Crooked ash-root haft capped with a swollen night-bloom bulb the size of a fist
  • Bulb skin is velvet-soft and clove-sweet; a stone pit waits under the petals
  • Leather wrist-loop braided from wilted stamens · the bloom never quite stops quivering
  • Squeeze the bulb — yields like fruit, then meets a hard core that will not give
  • Swing through air — almost no whistle; the mass arrives late with a soft whumpf
GM: Mercy Mallet — sedate / knock out; drop one triage step.
Object
Dewgut Choir
  • Stoppered glass, mug-sized, heavier than empty glass should be
  • Inside: a dozen fat, segmented dew-guts with iridescent side-flaps — half snail, half siphon
  • They leave wet silver trails · condensation beads wherever they climb
  • Smell of rain on warm stone and a hint of copper
  • Warm hand near the glass — they surge toward heat and begin a thin continuous eee, like wet fingers on crystal
GM: Thirstlings / leech-kin — drain blood, humor, fever-magic.
Object
Magpie’s Ice-Foot
  • Palm-sized brass body grown around a spool of pale silk line (stolen wedding-thread, if you ask the brass)
  • Line ends in three crystal talons — a bird’s foot carved from winter ice that never melts
  • Talons are cold and faintly sticky · brass smells of oil and old storms
  • Turn the crank — the foot opens and closes: click, click, click
  • Open talons near iron — one high short ring, as if the crystal is hungry for metal
GM: Crystal Claw Reel — extract foreign bodies / shrapnel / lodged teeth.
Object
Mudmouth Tar
  • Lidded ceramic that stays warm when the room is not — painted with a smiling cow that has too many eyes
  • Inside: black-green paste that stretches in living strings from a notched wooden spatula
  • Smell of wet leaves and barnyard-sweet · labels in three scripts, all deliberately smudged
  • Dab a little on skin — it grips like a mouth and will not wipe clean without oil
GM: Mooncalf Pitch — seal / close wounds (fey dung resin). Don’t over-explain.

Tray · set B

Same tray energy. Still no purpose printed.

Object
Jubilee Cap
  • Soft white-gold mushroom, plum-wide, with a tiny wood spoon stained amber at the tip
  • Underside flutters if the air moves · stem weeps a clear bead if broken
  • Spores smell like crushed orchard fruit left in sun — sweet enough to make your eyes water
  • Squeeze gently — a puff of fine spores; something nearby wants to laugh when they land
GM: Gigglecap — feed; laughter required for the effect to land.
Object
Etiquette Blade
  • A blade the length of a short-sword pretending it belongs on a tea tray
  • Polish throws one hard white strip of lamp-light · full tang · leather grip with room to choke up
  • No blood groove — only faint hash marks near the ricasso, like a tally of apologies
  • Edge surgical-thin · spine thick enough to chop through what manners say should stay attached
  • Draw from the scabbard — one metallic rattle, then a silence that feels intentional
GM: Scandal Blade — amputate / cut away dead tissue.
Object
Nurse-Vine Ampoule
  • Transparent pouch, fist-sized when flat, hanging from a hollow living vine
  • Vine ends in a hollow bone needle · twist-cap at the top · nothing inside
  • Frayed neck-tag shows one crooked Common word: YES
  • Blow into the twist-cap — pouch swells, then sighs flat; the vine drinks air toward the needle
  • Shake — a tiny dry rustle from the vine wall, as if something once lived there
GM: Whatever-Drip / IV — player invents fluid contents; you rule.
Object
Kindjailer’s Briarvise
  • Hinged bracer of pale wood banded in brass, lined with felt pressed into a forearm’s ghost
  • Side-crank turns gears in a little window · too many briar-teeth on the inner ring
  • Looks like jewelry that learned cruelty and then apologized
  • Turn the crank — each click narrows the cuff a finger-width until whatever is inside cannot move
  • Open the hinge — sounds like a music-box run backward
