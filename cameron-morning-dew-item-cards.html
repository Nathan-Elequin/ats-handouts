Object in hand
Unmarked Length

Light enough to hold in one fist near the middle; the thinner end tapers to a blunt point that has been worn smooth. When you tap it on a table it makes a dull, dry tok. No markings. No metal. A faint green smell, like snapped twigs after rain.

Seat objects 1–4

What you notice when you pick them up. Nothing else is written down.

Object on the tray
Cloth-Headed Weight
A short handle of dark wood that fits one hand, ending in a fist-sized knob wrapped in many layers of stained cloth. The cloth smells of clove and old sweat. The head gives slightly if you squeeze it; the core underneath is hard. Swinging it makes almost no whistle — too soft, too slow. A leather loop at the butt hangs for a wrist.
Mercy Mallet — sedate; drop one triage step.
Object on the tray
Singing Jar
A clear, stoppered vessel the size of a large mug. Inside: a dozen fat, segmented creatures the length of a finger, each with tiny iridescent flaps along the sides. They climb the glass when a warm hand gets close and make a thin, continuous eee like wet fingers on crystal. The jar is heavier than it looks. Condensation beads inside.
Thirstlings / leeches — drain humors / fever-blood.
Object on the tray
Three-Fingered Crane
Brass body, palm-sized, with a crank on one side and a spool of pale silk line. At the line’s end: three crystal prongs that open and close when you turn the crank — click, click, click. The prongs are cold and slightly sticky. Pointing the device at iron nails makes the crystal ring once, high and short. Fits awkwardly in two hands: crank hand and aiming hand.
Crystal Claw — extract foreign bodies / shrapnel.
Object on the tray
Warm Pot of Night
A lidded ceramic pot that stays warm even when the room is not. Inside: a black-green paste that stretches in strings when you lift the wooden spatula. It smells like wet leaves and something barnyard-sweet. Labels in three scripts, all smudged. If it touches skin it grips and will not wipe clean without oil. The spatula has a notch worn by years of the same grip.
Mooncalf Pitch — seal wounds (fey dung resin).

Seat objects 5–8

Same rule: describe what your senses get. Application is your problem.

Object on the tray
Pale Cap with a Spoon
A soft mushroom, white-gold, about the width of a plum. The underside flutters if you breathe on it. When squeezed gently it releases a puff of fine spores that smell like crushed orchard fruit and make your own mouth want to smile for no reason. Comes with a tiny wooden spoon, stained at the tip. The stem leaks a clear, sticky bead if broken.
Gigglecap — feed; patient must laugh for effect.
Object on the tray
Too-Long Knife
A blade the length of a short-sword, polished so hard it reflects the ceiling lamps in a single white strip. Full tang, leather-wrapped grip for one hand with room for a second if you choke up. No blood groove — just a faint etched ruler of hash marks near the ricasso. Edge is surgical-thin; the spine is thick enough to chop. In the scabbard it rattles once, metallic and impatient.
Scandal Blade — amputate / cut away.
Object on the tray
Empty Bladder on a Vine
A transparent pouch, fist-sized when flat, hanging from a flexible hollow vine that ends in a hollow bone needle. The pouch has a twist-cap at the top and nothing inside — you can pinch it shut and watch it stay collapsed. Shaking it makes a tiny dry rustle from the vine’s inner wall. A frayed tag tied to the neck shows one word in Common, written crooked: YES.
Whatever-Drip — IV; player invents contents.
Object on the tray
Toothed Cuff
A hinged bracer of pale wood banded in brass, lined with felt that has been compressed into the shape of someone’s forearm. A side-crank turns gears you can see through a little window; each click tightens the cuff a finger-width. Too many teeth on the inner ring. When locked, it holds whatever is inside perfectly still. Opening it sounds like a music-box run backward.
Gearvise — splint / set / brace.
